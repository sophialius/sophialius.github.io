CATS PLAYING MAHJONG


OVERVIEW

In small groups of 2 or 3, students will work together to establish a system that individual students will then utilize to recreate an image with Binary, ASCII, or Unicode characters. Groups may reconvene to reassess their group’s systems or individual students may “fork the code” (within reason) to better serve their images. Final text images must be a minimum of 60 character rows/columns along the shortest edge.

The final project's system requires: utilization of Mahjong Tile characters, a call to Chinese culture, and a freedom with color.

THE JESTER'S PRIVILEGE


OVERVIEW

The class will work together to build an assemblage sculpture from found and classroom materials. Individual students will then create a “CSS Still Life” that must consider HTML layout, nesting, and CSS styling. Final work may employ CSS animations and any degree of design reduction and abstraction. All HTML & CSS drawings must be horizontally oriented at 800x600px.

I reimagined a colorful, chaotic, and clown-centric scene.

I AM A KATAMARI FAN


OVERVIEW

Students will create a self-contained, single-page site that employs hover effects and CSS selectors to assist and encourage user interaction. Your site must include a minimum of 5 images (observing optimization and best practices) and all elements must contain adequate metadata/alt text. Your site must also account for viewport sizes to serve comparable or alternate user experiences.

I had recently discovered Katamari. It's only logical I created a fansite.

MY LOVE FOR YOU GROWS WITH TIME


OVERVIEW

Computational science and, by proxy, web design have a unique history tethered to the users system clock. Students will follow a formalized process from brainstorming and sketching to prototyping and construction to create a browser-based design that employs the system clock’s 3 basic events (seconds, minutes, hours) using p5.js.

My heart beats for you. And only you. So yours better beat for me. And only me.

A WEIRD CHICKEN NOODLE SOUP RECIPE


OVERVIEW

Students will design and write a program or script to be performed. This can be taken literally, as in your script is intended to be enlivened by actors. This can be taken loosely and all terms can be creatively interpreted. Regardless, all performances must incorporate learned principles of scripted “functions,” and must also include an element of “chance.”

joygaze


OVERVIEW

In teams of two (+), students will work together to create and execute an interactive design tool that employs API linking with IFTTT (If This Then That). Teams must also design and develop a website that serves to promote this tool and document its real-life application and usage.

WE ARE ALL CLOWNS


OVERVIEW

Using p5.js, students will program a generative design that employs functions, loops, and conditionals. Designs must also incorporate an element of randomness or chance in their code. Students will work to embed this design within a separate website that also incorporates designed and user-interactive elements.
